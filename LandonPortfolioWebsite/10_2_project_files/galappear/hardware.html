Hexapad

My Hexapad Controller is a piezo-based microphone array. I intended for it to be used as a controller, like a MIDI controller, but for audio in many different types of applications as opposed to being used for one specific composition. It was constructed out of layers of acrylic and birch wood cut by a laser cutter and stacked layer after layer held together by screws; it is quite robust. (I have dropped it many times...)

Hexapad


The Hexapad uses a total of 10 piezo microphones, each on their own mono channel that can be connected to with a ts/trs cable. This makes the Hexapad a 10-channel instrument! The piezos are distributed in the shape of hexagons for a concise arrangement. Each piezo is located under a neoprene pad about half an inch thick that helps isolate each piezo from the vibrations of adjacent piezos when struck. The neoprene pads also prevent direct contact with the piezos, as they can be quite sensitive and breakable, which prevents clicks and pops. It can be interacted with in any way imaginable, but I use my fingers to strike the pads like drum pads on a MIDI controller and by brushing the pads with combs and brushes; this gives very interesting audio signal that sound granular in nature. The Hexapad is great to use with MaxMSP to capture and manipulate the audio by DSP processes. In my case, I have used the pads to excite digital waveguides and loop pitch-shifted brush sounds.
